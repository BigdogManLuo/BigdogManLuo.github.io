Learning-based Optimization in Seaport Power-Logistics System

The operation of modern seaports features a strong coupling between energy supply and logistics demand. Optimizing such energy-logistics scheduling problems is typically formulated as large-scale mixed-integer programming, which is computationally expensive and must be solved repeatedly on a daily basis. Our work proposes a decision surrogate model for integer variable prediction in centralized scheduling and warm-start model for distributed scheduling in seaport power-logistics coupled scheduling. Both approaches improve computational efficiency while ensuring feasibility of the scheduling results, and the warm-start approach additionally guarantees the optimality.
